Select a resolution required code from the list below to see the appropriate solution for either port-ins or port-outs.

Telegence (TLG) performs a series of checks to validate and activate the cellular number(s) in the port-in request. If one of these checks do not pass, the port will fail to activate in TLG.

Solution:

- Create an AMDOCS Ticket.

There is no need to contact Tier 2.

ServiceNow Ticket:

After creating an AMDOCS ticket, use the appropriate verbiage below to finish the ticket in ServiceNow.

  • Short Description
      • CTN: Port In Activation Failed in TLG.
  • Description
      • AMDOCS - Please replay activation of port request # for CTN on BAN

ServiceNow Classification:

Exceed Article for Reference:

    ROS: Port In Activation Failed in TLG

"IC Timer Expired", typically indicates an issue with the old service provider's (OSP) system. Either it's heavily loaded or there is a system issue that's preventing a response after 30 minutes.

Solution:

  • Contact the OSP to provide a manual response.
    • Advise the AR to not cancel and resubmit port request. AR must wait for the system to clear and for a response to be generated.
  • If the OSP cannot generate a manual response, advise the AR and customer to wait an additional 30 minutes for an update on the port request(s).
    • After 30 minutes, if there is no response, advise the AR to cancel and resubmit the port request(s).

Definition: 6B will generate if port request has an incorrect or missing number transfer pin (NTP). This is to protect against unauthorized port out activity.

Select an option below:

Solution:

Contact the customer's current service provider to obtain the correct number transfer pin (NTP). Once the NTP is obtained, advise the AR to update the port-in request in Aktivate.

    Major Companies which need an NTP:
    • T-Mobile
      • Advise customer to dial 611 on their device to request a number transfer pin. Customer will be required to follow verification steps.
      • ROS: Resolution Required - 6B - Number Transfer Pin Required or Incorrect
    • AT&T
      • Advise customer to call *PORT on their AT&T device and follow prompts to receive NTP
      • Use the myATT app on the customer's device. Navigate to Profile and select People & Permissions to request a new transfer pin.
      • Contact ATT's porting department if customer needs further assistance obtaining the NTP
    • Verizon
      • A 6 digit transfer pin is required porting out from Verizon. Advise customer to dial #PORT from their Verizon Device or obtain the NTP via the My Verizon App.
      • Contact Verizon's porting department if customer needs further assistance obtaining the NTP.
      • Porting: Transferring from Verizon (RR 6B)
    Dealing with a Verizon Number Lock
    • Advise the customer to turn off number lock feature via the My Verizon App.
    • In the My Verizon App, open the Menu and tap Account.
    • Tap Account Settings.
    • Tap Number Lock.
    • Tap the toggle to turn off Number Lock.
    • Once the Number Lock is removed, advise the AR to resubmit the port request.
    • Porting: Transferring from Verizon (RR 6B)

Verbiage:

When contacting the OSP use verbiage below.

"I am calling from the Cricket Wireless Porting Department on a recorded line. I have a customer who is porting their number to Cricket and I need assistance with a 6B number transfer pin required or incorrect. I also have the customer available and can conference them onto the call at your request. The customer’s telephone number is _____"

A 6B can also trigger if there is an account lock on a Cricket Wireless account. The account lock feature can be enabled on the account.

Verify the Account Lock is enabled in Voyage.

Solution:

  • Customer must turn off account lock via the myCricketApp.
    • The account lock cannot be removed by the helpdesk, an AR or care. If the customer cannot remove the account lock, they must speak with care to have a case created to have the account lock removed.
  • Provide the customer the following instructions to remove account lock.
    • Navigate into the myCricket App and select Settings (Gear icon on the top right).
    • Under Security, click Account Lock.
    • Click Continue.
    • An OTP will be sent to the customer's mobile device. It will prompt the customer to enter the OTP onto the verification screen in the app to turn off the account lock.
    • Once the account lock is disabled, the port request can be resubmitted.

    ROS: Resolution Required - 6B - Remove Account Lock at Cricket Wireless

Definition: 6G - Port Complexity

This resolution required means the port is in delay, awaiting for a response from their current service provider.

Solution:

    Contact the OSP
    • Request for a manual response on the port request.
    • Ask if there are any issues on their end that may be causing the delay.
    • If a manual response cannot be initiated due to the service provider's automated porting system, advise the AR and customer to please wait 24-48 hours for an automated response on the port request.
    • If an automated response has not been generated after 48 hours, the port request will need to be cancelled and resubmitted.
    ROS: Resolution Required - 6G - Port Complexity

Verbiage:

When contacting the OSP use verbiage below.

"I am calling from the Cricket Wireless Porting Department on a recorded line. I have a customer who is porting their number to Cricket Wireless and I need assistance with a 6G Port Delay. I also have the customer available and can conference them onto the call at your request. The customer’s telephone number is _____"

Definition: 6M - Duplicate port request with another service provider.

Solution:

    Another port request exists with another service provider.
    Advise the AR to confirm with the customer whether they have attempted to port the number to another carrier within the last 30 days.
    • If a pending port request with another carrier exists, advise the customer they will need to contact the carrier and cancel the port request.
    • If customer is unsure if there is a pending port request, advise the customer to contact their current service provider to verify if there are any active port out requests on the account.
    Once the other pending port request is cancelled, the port in request to Cricket can be modified and resubmitted.
    ROS: Resolution Required - 6M - Duplicate Port Request with Another Service Provider

Definition: 6N - Duplicate request - port request is already pending.

This means there is a duplicate pending port request in the system for the cellular number.

    There are several instances which will trigger a 6N - Duplicate port request. Below will be the most common solutions for these instances.
    Please visit exceed article for further details.
    • ROS Porting: Port-In Resolution Required 6N

Solution:

    Common Solutions:
    • Contact Tier 2 to first cancel the prior port request before continuing to cancel the latest request receiving a 6N resolution required.
    • Await for further instructions from Tier 2. RHD may need to contact the OSP to verify for certain a cancellation is seen on their end. Resubmitting a new port request after verification of cancellation on the OSP's end may be necessary to clear the 6N from the system.
    Dealing with Verizon Numbers:
    • Verify the NLSP and NNSP in SMG.
    • If the NNSP does not match the Cricket SPID of X508 under the NLSP, this indicates there is a duplicate port request. See reference picture below
    • Advise the customer to contact the related carrier which has the customer's pending port request and advise to cancel the request on their behalf.
    • Tier 2 does not need to be contacted for this unless an issue arises which needs further review.
    • Visit Exceed Article for more information: ROS Porting: Port-In Resolution Required 6N

Definition: 6P - MDN has service provider port protection

This means the cellular number being ported has port protection enabled on their current service provider's network. Port protection is a feature customers can enable to protect against unauthorized porting activity on their cellular number.

Solution:

    Resoluton for other carriers:
    • Advise the customer to contact their current service provider to assist in removing the port protection feature from their account.
    • Once the port protection is removed, advise the AR they will need to resubmit the port request.
    Dealing with a Verizon Number Lock
    • Advise the customer to turn off number lock feature via the My Verizon App.
    • In the My Verizon App, open the Menu and tap Account.
    • Tap Account Settings.
    • Tap Number Lock.
    • Tap the toggle to turn off Number Lock.
    • Once the Number Lock is removed, advise the AR to resubmit the port request.
    • Porting: Transferring from Verizon (RR 6B)
